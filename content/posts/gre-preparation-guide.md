# GRE Complete Preparation Guide: Test Dates, Study Plan, and Scoring Strategies for 2025

The GRE (Graduate Record Examination) is a standardized test required for admission to most graduate schools in the United States. Whether you're planning to pursue a master's degree or doctoral program, achieving a competitive GRE score is essential. This comprehensive guide will help you navigate GRE preparation in 2025.

## What is the GRE?

The GRE General Test measures your verbal reasoning, quantitative reasoning, critical thinking, and analytical writing skills - skills that have been developed over a long period and are not related to a specific field of study.

## GRE Test Structure

### Computer-Delivered GRE (Most Common)

**Analytical Writing** (60 minutes)
- Two tasks: Issue Task and Argument Task
- Scored separately from 0-6 in half-point increments

**Verbal Reasoning** (two sections, 30 minutes each)
- 20 questions per section
- Tests reading comprehension, text completion, and sentence equivalence

**Quantitative Reasoning** (two sections, 35 minutes each)
- 20 questions per section
- Tests basic math, algebra, geometry, and data analysis

**Unscored Section**
- May appear as an additional Verbal or Quant section
- Used for test development purposes
- Not identified, so complete all sections

**Research Section**
- Appears at the end in some tests
- Used for research and not scored
- Identified on screen

### Paper-Delivered GRE
Available in areas where computer-delivered testing is not available.

## GRE Scoring

### Score Ranges

**Analytical Writing**: 0-6 (in 0.5 point increments)
**Verbal Reasoning**: 130-170 (in 1-point increments)
**Quantitative Reasoning**: 130-170 (in 1-point increments)

**Total Score**: 260-340 (sum of Verbal and Quant scores)

### Score Percentiles

**Verbal/Quant Scores**:
- 170: 99th percentile
- 165: 96th percentile
- 160: 88th percentile
- 155: 75th percentile
- 150: 50th percentile

**Average Scores by Field**:
- **Engineering**: V155, Q161
- **Physical Sciences**: V153, Q160
- **Life Sciences**: V152, Q153
- **Business**: V150, Q153
- **Humanities**: V159, Q148
- **Education**: V152, Q147

## 6-Month GRE Study Plan

### Months 1-2: Diagnostic and Content Review

**Week 1-2: Take a Diagnostic Test**
- Use an official ETS practice test
- Identify your baseline scores
- Determine which sections need the most work

**Week 3-8: Build Foundation**
- Review math concepts (algebra, geometry, data analysis)
- Study vocabulary (aim for 1,000-1,500 high-frequency words)
- Practice reading comprehension techniques
- Analyze argumentative writing structure

**Resources to Use**:
- Official ETS GRE Study Guide
- Magoosh GRE vocabulary app
- Khan Academy for math review
- The Economist and Scientific American for reading practice

### Months 3-4: Practice and Improvement

**Week 9-12: Section-Specific Practice**
- Take section practice tests 3-4 times per week
- Review incorrect answers thoroughly
- Build vocabulary through context
- Practice analytical writing (complete 4-6 Issue and Argument tasks)

**Week 13-16: Full-Length Practice**
- Take 2-3 full-length practice tests
- Simulate test day conditions (stricter timing, no breaks)
- Track your progress and identify remaining weak areas
- Refine test-taking strategies

### Months 5-6: Fine-Tuning and Test Day Prep

**Week 17-20: Intensive Practice**
- Take practice tests weekly
- Review every question you get wrong
- Focus on time management strategies
- Refine your vocabulary list

**Week 21-24: Final Preparation**
- Take a final practice test 1-2 weeks before test day
- Review key formulas and concepts
- Relax and maintain confidence
- Prepare required documents and ID

## GRE Section-Specific Strategies

### Verbal Reasoning

**Text Completion** (25% of questions):
- Read the sentence with blanks first
- Identify clue words (however, although, therefore, etc.)
- Predict the answer before looking at options
- Use process of elimination

**Reading Comprehension** (50% of questions):
- Skim the passage first to understand main ideas
- Read questions to know what to look for
- Pay attention to the author's tone and attitude
- Base answers only on information in the passage

**Sentence Equivalence** (25% of questions):
- Understand the overall meaning of the sentence
- Both answer choices must complete the sentence logically
- Look for context clues and transition words

### Quantitative Reasoning

**Key Topics**:
- Arithmetic (properties, powers/roots, properties of integers)
- Algebra (equations, inequalities, functions)
- Geometry (area, volume, coordinate geometry)
- Data Analysis (statistics, probability, data interpretation)

**Strategies**:
- Memorize key formulas (area, volume, quadratic formula)
- Use the on-screen calculator for complex calculations
- Approximate when exact calculations aren't necessary
- backsolve for algebraic problems
- Draw diagrams for geometry problems

### Analytical Writing

**Issue Task (30 minutes)**:
- Choose a side and support with specific examples
- Structure: Introduction, 2-3 body paragraphs, conclusion
- Address counterarguments for a stronger essay

**Argument Task (30 minutes)**:
- Analyze the logical structure of the argument
- Identify assumptions and logical fallacies
- Suggest improvements or additional evidence needed

**Tips for Success**:
- Plan before writing (5-7 minutes)
- Use specific examples and evidence
- Stay on topic and address the prompt directly
- Leave time to review and make corrections

## Test Day Preparation

### What to Bring

✓ **Valid ID** (driver's license or passport)
✓ **Confirmation email** printout or ETS account
✓ **Appropriate attire** (test centers can be cold)
✓ **Snacks and water** for break time
✓ **Earplugs** (foam earplugs allowed if testing room is noisy)

### What NOT to Bring

✗ **Mobile phones** (must be turned off and stored)
✗ **Smartwatches or fitness trackers**
✗ **Calculators** (provided on-screen for quant section)
✗ **Books, notes, or papers**
✗ **Pencils or pens** (use provided whiteboard for scratch work)

### During the Test

1. **Take notes** on the provided whiteboard during breaks
2. **Manage your time** carefully - 30-35 minutes per section
3. **Skip difficult questions** and return to them
4. **Use the on-screen calculator** wisely
5. **Review flagged questions** if time permits

## GRE vs GMAT: Which Should You Take?

**Choose GRE if you are:**
- Applying to diverse programs (business, arts, sciences)
- Strong in verbal reasoning
- Looking for more flexibility in test timing
- Want to apply to schools that accept both tests

**Choose GMAT if you are:**
- Applying exclusively to business school
- Want a test specifically designed for business
- Prefer data sufficiency questions over geometry
- Have unlimited retake attempts budgeted

## Common GRE Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Not reading questions carefully** - especially in quantitative comparison
2. **Spending too long on difficult questions** - skip and return later
3. **Over-relying on calculators** - practice mental math
4. **Ignoring vocabulary** - consistent daily practice is key
5. **Not timing yourself** during practice - get comfortable with pacing
6. **Skipping analytical writing practice** - it counts toward your score
7. **Taking the test too early** without adequate preparation

## Sending Scores to Schools

- **Cost**: $27 per school
- **Timing**: Scores are available 10-15 days after the test
- **Free Reports**: 4 score reports included with registration
- **Additional Reports**: Can be sent anytime for a fee

## Retaking the GRE

**Wait Period**: 21 days between test attempts
**Maximum**: Once every 21 days, up to 5 times in a 12-month period
**Score Improvement**: Most students see 5-10 point improvement on retakes

**When to Retake**:
- You're more than 10 points below your target score
- You've had time to address weak areas (1+ months)
- You're applying to highly competitive programs
- Your first score was significantly below practice test results

## Final Preparation Tips

1. **Create a realistic study schedule** and stick to it
2. **Take full-length practice tests** under real conditions
3. **Review every mistake** until you understand it completely
4. **Build stamina** by taking multiple sections in sequence
5. **Practice writing essays** under time pressure
6. **Get adequate sleep** - especially the week before the test
7. **Stay confident** - trust your preparation
